Campaign objective

Genza's goal for this campaign is to validate creator-led distribution as an acquisition channel ahead of a larger US push. The product is launching its app and its influencer marketing simultaneously — with no prior creator campaigns to learn from.

This test is designed to generate that learning: which platforms, creator profiles, and creative angles produce qualified, low-cost installs for Genza in the US.

Performance ads alone are expensive and easy to ignore in the dating/social space. Creators give Genza what paid media can't buy at launch: native context, social proof, and a playful first impression at scale.

"The goal isn't reach. It's proving — cheaply and quickly — that the right creators and the right angle can turn curiosity into qualified installs."

Not a dating app campaign. Genza is positioned as a playful social game — protecting brand safety while matching how Gen Z actually behaves.

Every placement is an experiment. High creator volume produces creative variation; we scale what converts.

18+ audience only. All targeting, creator selection, and creative is built around an adult audience.

Target audience

Gen Z adults (18+) in the US — students and young adults who download things because they're fun.

1
College students (18–22)
On-campus young adults building new social circles. Heavy TikTok use, strong word-of-mouth behavior. The natural early-adopter core.
2
Young urban adults (22–27)
Working young adults in LA and NY, socially active, trend-aware, comfortable trying new apps. Higher disposable attention for novelty and games.
3
Bilingual / Spanish-speaking adults
A large, engaged segment concentrated in Los Angeles. Best reached through Spanish-language creators and longer-form YouTube content.

Why influencer marketing fits Genza

Four structural reasons creator-led distribution is the right channel for a brand-new social app entering a competitive US market.

01
A brand-new product needs trusted introductions
Genza has no brand equity in the US yet. Creators provide an instant, credible introduction — a familiar voice vouching for a new app — which lowers the trust barrier that a cold paid ad can't.
02
Playful mechanics are made to be shown, not described
The AI character, the farm game, and the "AI picks your match" reveal are visual, fun, and inherently shareable. Creator content shows the experience in action far better than a banner or pre-roll.
03
TikTok rewards native, creator-led formats
Genza's social-game angle fits TikTok's native patterns — POV, skits, reactions, duets. Creator content can earn organic distribution beyond the paid placement when the format lands.
04
Creator content fuels paid amplification later
The best-performing creator videos become ready-made ad creative for TikTok Spark Ads and Instagram Partnership Ads in the next phase — so this test also produces a creative library, not just installs.

Creative concept

Genza is sold as a playful social game with an AI character at its center — not a dating app. Every placement opens with curiosity and entertainment, then routes interested viewers to a low-friction install. The promise: fun and novelty first, connection second.

1
The AI character
A personality people want to watch and react to. The curiosity hook that stops the scroll.
2
"AI picked my match" reveal
An instant, on-camera payoff creators can perform. The entertainment hook that drives shares.
3
Launch perk for new users
In-app reward / early-access status that gives the creator a concrete reason to say "download now."

Creative hypotheses — 4 angles to test

HYPOTHESIS 01
"The AI that lives in the real world"
POV / skit content where the AI character does human things, reacts to the real world, and is "looking for connection." Hook is curiosity, not dating. Tests: does the character drive curiosity-led installs?
TikTok Comedy/POV creators
HYPOTHESIS 02
"Raise your match" — Tamagotchi angle
Lead with the in-app farm/pet game and daily quests. Frame Genza as a cozy social game you check daily, where matching is a fun layer. Tests: does game-first framing convert cozy-game audiences?
TikTok YouTube Cozy-game creators
HYPOTHESIS 03
"I let an AI pick my match"
Creators take the questionnaire on camera and react to who the AI matches them with — playful, reaction-driven, low-stakes. Strong duet/stitch potential. Tests: does the reveal mechanic drive shares?
TikTok Comedy/lifestyle creators
HYPOTHESIS 04
"The app everyone on campus is trying"
Social-proof / FOMO angle from college creators — it's the fun new thing friends are doing together. Positions Genza as a group activity, not a solo dating download. Tests: does social proof drive the student core?
TikTok Instagram College creators
